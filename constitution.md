This comprehensive Non-Governmental Organization (NGO) constitution template is designed to meet the requirements for registration in South Sudan, in compliance with the Non-Governmental Organizations Act, 2016. It establishes your organization's legal and operational framework.Article 1: Name and LocationName: The organization shall be called [Name of the Organization] (hereinafter referred to as the "Organization").Headquarters: The main office shall be located in [City, e.g., Juba], Republic of South Sudan. Branch offices may be established in other states or counties as approved by the Board of Directors.Article 2: Vision, Mission, and ObjectivesVision: [Insert your long-term desired impact, e.g., A peaceful and prosperous society where all vulnerable communities have access to basic needs and rights.]Mission: [Insert your primary purpose and who you serve.]Objectives:To [Insert specific objective 1, e.g., provide humanitarian aid and emergency relief to vulnerable populations.]To [Insert specific objective 2, e.g., promote peacebuilding, human rights awareness, and community development.]To advocate for and empower marginalized groups, specifically women, children, and youth.Article 3: MembershipCategories of Membership: Membership shall be open to individuals who subscribe to the objectives of the Organization and may include:Ordinary Members: South Sudanese citizens who actively support the organization.Honorary Members: Individuals recognized by the Board for their outstanding contribution to the Organization.Termination: Membership may be terminated by resignation, death, or expulsion for conduct deemed detrimental to the aims of the Organization.Article 4: Governance and StructureGeneral Assembly: The supreme governing body of the Organization, comprising all registered members. It meets annually at the Annual General Meeting (AGM).Board of Directors: The governing and oversight body responsible for policy-making and strategic direction. It shall consist of between [e.g., 5 and 9] members elected by the General Assembly, including the Chairperson, Secretary, and Treasurer.Secretariat (Management): The executive arm responsible for the day-to-day running of the Organization, headed by the Executive Director (or Secretary-General).Article 5: Duties of Office BearersChairperson: Shall convene and preside over all General and Board meetings, and act as the official spokesperson for the Organization.Secretary: Shall record and keep minutes of all meetings, handle official correspondence, and maintain the register of members.Treasurer: Shall oversee all financial operations, ensure proper bookkeeping, and present financial reports at the AGM.Article 6: Financial Management and AccountabilityFunding: The Organization shall be funded by membership dues, grants, donations, and fundraising activities.Financial Year: The financial year shall run from [e.g., 1st January] to [e.g., 31st December].Bank Accounts: Accounts shall be opened in reputable commercial banks in South Sudan, with signatories designated by the Board of Directors.Auditing: The accounts of the Organization shall be audited annually by an independent, certified external auditor appointed by the General Assembly.Article 7: Employment and StaffingThe Organization shall comply with the South Sudan NGO Act, 2016 by ensuring that no less than 80 percent of employees are South Sudanese nationals across managerial, middle, and junior levels. Expatriate staff shall only be engaged if there are no qualified South Sudanese nationals available for the roles.Article 8: Amendment of the ConstitutionThis constitution may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of members present and voting at an Annual General Meeting or a Special General Meeting, provided that notice of the proposed amendments has been circulated at least [e.g., 21 days] prior to the meeting.Article 9: DissolutionThe Organization may be dissolved by a resolution passed by a three-quarters (3/4) majority vote of members present at a Special General Meeting convened for that purpose.Upon dissolution, and after all debts and liabilities have been paid, the remaining assets and property shall not be distributed among members. They shall be transferred to another legally recognized organization with similar objectives in South Sudan.
