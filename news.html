Jdiobe STEM Foundation awards travel scholarship to future aeronautical engineer

By Jdiobe STEM Foundation 5 min read

At a time when travel costs can stand between talent and opportunity, our foundation stepped in to help a promising student begin a life-changing academic journey.

Why this matters

The Jdiobe STEM Foundation is proud to announce the award of a $1,000 travel scholarship to Moses Nambiro, a student from Uganda who has been admitted and awarded a scholarship to pursue a Bachelor of Science in Aeronautical Engineering. This support helps remove a major obstacle that often stands between gifted students and world-class education.

While Nambiro successfully secured admission and academic funding, additional financial support was required to cover travel expenses. Through this scholarship, the Jdiobe STEM Foundation is supporting his airfare from Uganda to Alegría, enabling him to begin his academic journey with greater confidence and less financial strain.

This award reflects the Foundation’s mission to remove barriers and expand access to STEM education for talented individuals globally. By addressing critical gaps such as travel costs, the Foundation ensures that opportunity is not limited by circumstance and that young people can pursue ambitious futures in science and engineering.

“We believe that talent is universal, but opportunity is not. Supporting students like Nambiro is central to our mission of empowering the next generation of engineers and innovators.”

— Dr. Muwanika Jdiobe

The Jdiobe STEM Foundation congratulates Moses Nambiro on this achievement and looks forward to his future contributions to the field of aeronautical engineering.

New scholarship support for Mbabazi Liz Lourine at Holy Cross Lake View SSS

By Jdiobe STEM Foundation 4 min read

For Liz Lourine, this new support means more than financial help; it opens a path to confidence, opportunity, and growth in science-led learning through the Dr. Ron and Cara Beer STEM Scholarship.

What this support means

The Jdiobe STEM Foundation is pleased to share a new scholarship support story for Mbabazi Liz Lourine, a student at Holy Cross Lake View SSS in Jinja. She has been awarded a scholarship through JdiobeSTEM Foundation under the Dr. Ron and Cara Beer STEM Scholarship, supporting her studies in BAF - Biology, Agriculture, Food and Nutrition / SubICT, a course that strengthens practical knowledge and future opportunities in science and applied learning.

This new support reflects our continued commitment to helping young people access quality education, build confidence, and grow their potential through STEM-related learning and academic encouragement. It is a reminder that practical support, when paired with opportunity, can transform a student’s future.
