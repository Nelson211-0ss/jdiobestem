The Jdiobe STEM Foundation was founded with a simple but powerful mission: to ignite curiosity and opportunity through STEM education in communities that need it most. Legally incorporated in the United States, the Foundation operates its programs on the ground in Uganda, where the founder, Dr. Muwanika Jdiobe, was born and first dreamed of making science and innovation accessible to all.

Dr. Jdiobe’s journey from the classrooms of Uganda to earning a Ph.D. in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering in the United States, inspired a vision to give back. He recognized a deep gap in access to modern STEM education, hands-on research opportunities, and mentorship for Ugandan youth.

What began as personal outreach, hosting workshops, guiding students through science fair projects, and organizing school visits, quickly grew into a movement. In response to increasing demand and a growing network of students and educators, the Jdiobe STEM Foundation was officially launched as a nonprofit organization.

Since then, it has expanded its impact across Uganda through programs in robotics, aerospace education, water monitoring research, and environmental engineering. We emphasize not just learning, but doing; empowering students to participate in real-world research and solve local problems using STEM tools.

With its unique cross-border structure, registered in the U.S. and operating in Uganda; the Foundation bridges continents, resources, and ideas. Our work continues to grow through partnerships with schools, universities, and communities, all united by the belief that brilliance exists everywhere, but opportunity must be built.

Dr. Muwanika Jdiobe

President & Founder

Aerospace & mechanical engineering · Aircraft noise & acoustics

Dr. Muwanika Jdiobe is a distinguished aerospace engineer and President of the Jdiobe STEM Foundation. His passion for innovation began in Uganda, where he built alternating current generators and simple helicopters using local materials during his secondary school years.

He holds dual Bachelor’s degrees in Aerospace and Mechanical Engineering, a Master’s, and a Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering from Oklahoma State University. His research includes aeroacoustic characterization of coaxial ducted propellers and autonomous systems like MANUEL, an autonomous boat for environmental monitoring.

Currently, Dr. Jdiobe works at The Boeing Company as an aerospace engineer specializing in aircraft noise prediction and control. He is an active member of technical committees, including AIAA’s Aeroacoustics Technical Committee and NASA’s Acoustics Technical Working Group.

Through the Jdiobe STEM Foundation, he continues to inspire young engineers and scientists, demonstrating the power of STEM education to drive innovation and transformation.
