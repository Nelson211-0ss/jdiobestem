1.2 The Foundation may be referred to by the abbreviation **"JSF"** and may publish, communicate and operate under the name "Jdiobe STEM Foundation" in every jurisdiction in which it is lawfully registered.

1.3 **Headquarters.** The main office of the Foundation shall be located in **Juba**, Central Equatoria State, Republic of South Sudan, at [INSERT FULL PHYSICAL ADDRESS], or at such other place within South Sudan as the Board of Directors may determine.

1.4 **Branch offices** may be established in other states or counties of the Republic of South Sudan as approved by the Board of Directors and upon obtaining the requisite approvals from the Relief and Rehabilitation Commission and the relevant state and county authorities.

1.5 **Intellectual property in the name.** The name, logo, brand marks, colors and domain names of the Foundation are the exclusive property of the Foundation. No person shall use them without the prior written authorization of the Board of Directors.

1.6 The Foundation shall not change its name except by Special Resolution of the General Assembly and with the prior written approval of the Relief and Rehabilitation Commission and any other competent authority.

## ARTICLE 3 — LEGAL STATUS

3.1 The Foundation is a **non-governmental, non-profit-making, non-partisan, non-sectarian and voluntary organization**.

3.2 The Foundation shall register and maintain in good standing with the **Relief and Rehabilitation Commission** under the Act, and shall obtain and renew all certificates, permits and approvals required before undertaking any activity in the Republic of South Sudan.

3.3 The Foundation shall additionally register with the Ministry of Justice and Constitutional Affairs, the National Revenue Authority, and such state and county authorities as the law requires.

3.4 The Foundation is a body corporate with perpetual succession, capable in its own name of suing and being sued, of acquiring, holding and disposing of property, and of doing all such acts as bodies corporate may lawfully do.

3.5 **International affiliation.** The Foundation maintains an affiliated office in Oklahoma City, United States of America, which supports fundraising, donor stewardship and international partnerships. That office operates under the law of its own jurisdiction, and:
- (a) no activity of the affiliated office shall commit the Foundation to any act contrary to the Act or to this Constitution;
- (b) all funds received from or through the affiliated office for South Sudan programs shall be routed through the Foundation's registered accounts and reported to the Commission as the Act requires; and
- (c) the Board shall ensure that the relationship between the arms is recorded in a written memorandum and disclosed to the Commission.

3.6 **Limited liability.** No Member shall be personally liable for the debts or obligations of the Foundation save to the extent of any subscription lawfully due and unpaid, or where liability arises from that Member's own fraud, wilful misconduct or gross negligence.

# PART II — PURPOSE

## ARTICLE 6 — VISION, MISSION AND CORE VALUES

### 6.1 Vision

> A society where every student, regardless of background, circumstance or displacement, has the opportunity to access quality STEM education and to contribute to solving local and global challenges.

### 6.2 Mission

> To empower underserved students through access to STEM education, scholarships, mentorship, and innovation-driven opportunities that prepare them to become future leaders and problem solvers.

### 6.3 Core Values

The Foundation and every person acting in its name shall be guided by the following values:

- **(a) Access** — capable students should not be limited by financial barriers, geography, displacement, or lack of exposure.
- **(b) Mentorship** — guidance from educators, professionals and role models can change the direction of a student's life.
- **(c) Innovation** — students should be encouraged to apply STEM knowledge to solve real problems in their communities and beyond.
- **(d) Service** — the Foundation exists to create lasting impact for students, families and communities, not for the benefit of its Members.
- **(e) Integrity and Accountability** — the Foundation shall be transparent with donors, beneficiaries, regulators and the public, and shall account fully for every resource entrusted to it.
- **(f) Dignity and Inclusion** — every beneficiary, member of Personnel and partner shall be treated with respect and without discrimination on any ground, including ethnicity.
- **(g) Safeguarding** — the safety and wellbeing of children and vulnerable persons takes precedence over all other interests of the Foundation.
- **(h) National Capacity** — the Foundation is committed to building and employing South Sudanese talent and to transferring skills at every level of the organization.

---

## ARTICLE 7 — OBJECTIVES

7.1 The objectives for which the Foundation is established are:

**(a) Scholarships and financial support** — to award scholarships, bursaries, grants, stipends and material support to academically promising and financially disadvantaged students pursuing STEM education at primary, secondary, vocational, undergraduate and postgraduate levels.

**(b) Youth STEM education** — to design and deliver hands-on STEM workshops, clubs, camps and curricula for primary and middle-school learners that inspire early curiosity in science, technology, engineering and mathematics.

**(c) Community STEM outreach** — to conduct workshops, demonstrations, science fairs, exhibitions and community events in partnership with schools, local leaders and community organizations, so that STEM learning extends beyond the classroom.

**(d) Secondary-school research** — to establish and support mentored student research programs, science and robotics competitions, and innovation challenges that build critical thinking, experimental skill and problem-solving capacity.

**(e) Mentorship and career readiness** — to connect students with engineers, scientists, educators, researchers and industry professionals through structured mentorship, internships, industry visits, and career-guidance programs.

**(f) Teacher and institutional capacity building** — to train teachers, equip laboratories and libraries, supply teaching and learning materials, and strengthen the capacity of schools and community institutions to deliver quality STEM education.

**(g) Aerospace and applied sciences** — to promote aerospace, robotics, environmental engineering, water quality monitoring and allied applied sciences through dedicated institutes, programs and facilities.

**(h) Girls, displaced learners and inclusion in STEM** — to design and implement targeted interventions that increase the participation, retention and success of girls, learners with disabilities, internally displaced persons, returnees, refugees and other under-represented groups in STEM.

**(i) Research, evidence and advocacy** — to conduct, commission and publish research on STEM education access and outcomes, and to engage constructively with policymakers, educators and the public in a lawful and non-partisan manner.

**(j) Partnerships** — to build and maintain partnerships with schools, universities, government ministries, development partners, foundations, corporations and individuals in South Sudan and internationally, in furtherance of the objectives above.

**(k) Resource mobilization** — to solicit, receive, hold, invest and disburse funds, grants, donations, endowments and gifts in kind for the advancement of the objectives of the Foundation.

**(l) National capacity development** — to build the STEM teaching, research and technical workforce of the Republic of South Sudan through training, employment and progressive transfer of responsibility to nationals.

**(m) Ancillary objects** — to do all such other lawful things as are incidental or conducive to the attainment of the foregoing objectives.

7.2 The objectives set out in Article 7.1 shall be pursued **exclusively for charitable, educational, humanitarian and public-benefit purposes** and for no other purpose.

7.3 No objective shall be construed as authorizing the Foundation to engage in any activity requiring a licence, permit or accreditation which the Foundation does not hold.

## ARTICLE 8 — POWERS OF THE ORGANIZATION

8.1 In furtherance of its objectives, and subject to the Act and all other applicable law, the Foundation shall have power to:

- (a) receive, solicit and accept donations, grants, subscriptions, bequests, endowments and gifts of money or property, whether from within or outside South Sudan, and to open, operate and close bank accounts in respect of the same;
- (b) purchase, lease, hire, hold, improve, manage, exchange, mortgage, charge, sell or otherwise dispose of movable and immovable property, subject to the law governing the holding of land by non-governmental organizations;
- (c) employ, engage, remunerate, discipline and dismiss staff, consultants, interns and volunteers, and to establish terms and conditions of service consistent with the Labour Act, 2017 and Article 30 of this Constitution;
- (d) enter into contracts, memoranda of understanding, grant agreements, partnership agreements and joint ventures consistent with its objectives;
- (e) establish, operate, affiliate with or support schools, institutes, laboratories, resource centres, libraries, incubation hubs and similar facilities;
- (f) award scholarships, prizes, grants, fellowships and stipends on such terms as the Board may determine;
- (g) publish, print, broadcast, distribute and disseminate materials, research, curricula and communications in any medium;
- (h) invest funds not immediately required, prudently and at low risk, in accordance with an investment policy approved by the Board;
- (i) borrow or raise money on terms approved by the Board, **provided that** the Foundation shall not encumber substantially all of its assets without the approval of the General Assembly;
- (j) affiliate with, join, or become a member of national, regional or international networks, federations, NGO forums and umbrella bodies with compatible objectives;
- (k) insure the Foundation, its property, its Personnel and its Board members against such risks as the Board considers prudent, including directors' and officers' liability and medical evacuation cover;
- (l) establish subsidiaries, chapters, affiliates or trusts where necessary, subject to regulatory approval; and
- (m) do all such other lawful acts as may be necessary, incidental or conducive to the attainment of its objectives.

8.2 **Restrictions on powers.** The Foundation shall **not**:

- (a) distribute any of its income, profits or assets to any Member, Board member, Office Bearer or member of Personnel by way of dividend, bonus or profit-share;
- (b) engage in partisan political activity, support or oppose any political party or candidate for public office, or use its resources for any partisan political purpose;
- (c) engage in any trade or business except where carried on in furtherance of its objectives and the proceeds are applied solely to those objectives;
- (d) carry out any activity outside the objectives stated in Article 7 or outside its approved area of operation;
- (e) accept any funding whose terms would compel the Foundation to act contrary to this Constitution, its safeguarding obligations, applicable law, or the interests of its beneficiaries;
- (f) engage in, facilitate or condone any form of discrimination, exploitation, abuse, corruption or money laundering;
- (g) engage in any activity that promotes or exacerbates ethnic, communal or political division; or
- (h) act in a manner prejudicial to the security, laws, sovereignty or dignity of the people of the Republic of South Sudan.

## ARTICLE 9 — NON-PROFIT, NON-PARTISAN AND NON-DISCRIMINATORY CHARACTER

9.1 The Foundation is established and shall be operated **not for profit**. The whole of its income and property, howsoever derived, shall be applied solely towards the promotion of its objectives.

9.2 **No private benefit.** No part of the income or property of the Foundation shall be paid or transferred, directly or indirectly, by way of dividend, bonus or otherwise by way of profit, to any Member, Board member or Office Bearer.

9.3 **Permitted payments.** Article 9.2 shall not prevent the payment in good faith of:
- (a) reasonable and proper remuneration to any Personnel for services actually rendered;
- (b) reimbursement of reasonable expenses properly incurred on the business of the Foundation and supported by receipts;
- (c) reasonable sitting allowances and travel costs to Board members for attendance at meetings, as approved in advance by the Board and disclosed in the annual accounts;
- (d) interest at a reasonable rate on money lent to the Foundation; and
- (e) reasonable rent for premises let to the Foundation on arm's-length terms,

**provided that** every such payment is approved in accordance with Article 31 (Conflict of Interest) and disclosed in the annual financial statements.

9.4 **Board service generally unremunerated.** Board members serve voluntarily and shall not receive salary or fees for Board service, save as permitted by Article 9.3(c). A Board member separately employed by the Foundation shall not participate in any decision concerning their own remuneration.

9.5 **Non-discrimination.** The Foundation shall not discriminate against any person in the provision of its services, the selection of beneficiaries, the recruitment of Personnel, or the admission of Members, on grounds of sex, gender, age, disability, race, ethnicity, tribe, clan, colour, national or social origin, religion or belief, health status including HIV status, displacement, refugee or returnee status, marital or family status, socio-economic status, or political opinion.

9.6 **Merit and need in beneficiary selection.** Selection of scholarship and program beneficiaries shall be based on published, objective criteria of academic merit, demonstrated financial need, and commitment to STEM, applied transparently and free from favouritism, nepotism, ethnic preference or solicitation of any inducement.

9.7 **Ethnic and geographic balance.** In recruitment, beneficiary selection and Board composition, the Foundation shall take reasonable steps to reflect the diversity of the communities it serves and to avoid the domination of any organ by a single ethnic group or region. The Board shall monitor and report on this annually.

9.8 **Non-partisan.** The Foundation shall maintain strict political neutrality. Personnel and Board members shall not use the name, resources, platforms or premises of the Foundation to advance any partisan political interest. Any person seeking elective political office shall disclose that fact to the Board and shall, where the Board so determines, take leave of absence or resign.

9.9 **Non-sectarian.** The Foundation shall not promote or require adherence to any religious belief as a condition of employment, membership or benefit.

9.10 **Humanitarian principles.** In any activity carried out in a humanitarian context, the Foundation shall observe the principles of humanity, neutrality, impartiality and independence.

# PART III — MEMBERSHIP

## ARTICLE 10 — CATEGORIES OF MEMBERSHIP

10.1 Membership shall be open to persons who subscribe to the objectives of the Foundation, and shall consist of the following categories:

**(a) Founder Members** — the persons who subscribed to this Constitution at its adoption and whose names appear in Article 42.2. Founder Members hold full voting rights and shall not be required to pay annual subscriptions unless the General Assembly resolves otherwise.

**(b) Ordinary Members** — South Sudanese citizens and other persons of eighteen (18) years or above who actively support the Foundation, apply in the prescribed form, are admitted by the Board, and pay the prescribed subscription. Ordinary Members hold full voting rights.

**(c) Associate Members** — individuals or organizations that support the Foundation but do not seek voting rights, including partner institutions, corporate supporters and international friends of the Foundation. Associate Members may attend and speak at the General Assembly but shall not vote or hold office.

**(d) Honorary Members** — individuals recognized by the Board, and confirmed by the General Assembly, for outstanding contribution to the Foundation or to STEM education. Honorary Members pay no subscription and shall not vote or hold office unless they also hold Ordinary Membership.

**(e) Youth/Student Members** — beneficiaries and alumni aged sixteen (16) years and above. Youth Members below eighteen (18) years require the written consent of a parent or guardian, shall not vote or hold office, and shall participate only in accordance with the Safeguarding Policy. On attaining eighteen (18) years a Youth Member may apply for Ordinary Membership.

10.2 The Board may, subject to ratification by the General Assembly, create additional categories of membership and prescribe their rights and obligations.

10.3 The total number of Members shall not be limited.

10.4 A majority of Members entitled to vote shall at all times be South Sudanese nationals.

---

## ARTICLE 11 — ADMISSION TO MEMBERSHIP

11.1 An applicant shall complete the prescribed **Membership Application Form** (Template A), including an undertaking to abide by this Constitution and the Code of Conduct.

11.2 Applications shall be considered by the Board, or by a Membership Committee to which the Board has delegated the function, at its next meeting or within sixty (60) days of receipt, whichever is earlier.

11.3 The Board may admit or decline an application. Where declined, the Board shall record its reasons in writing and the applicant may appeal to the next General Assembly, whose decision shall be final.

11.4 Membership takes effect on the date the applicant's name is entered in the Register of Members following approval and, where applicable, payment of the prescribed fee.

11.5 Membership is personal and shall not be transferred, assigned or inherited.

11.6 An institutional Associate Member shall nominate one representative in writing and notify the Secretary of any change.

11.7 **Subscriptions.** The General Assembly shall prescribe entrance fees and annual subscriptions for each category. Current rates shall be recorded in the Register of Members and in the minutes. Inability to pay shall not by itself bar membership where the Board grants a waiver on grounds of financial hardship.

## ARTICLE 12 — RIGHTS AND OBLIGATIONS OF MEMBERS

12.1 **Every Member has the right to:**
- (a) participate in the activities and programs of the Foundation;
- (b) receive notice of, attend, and speak at General Assembly meetings;
- (c) access the Constitution, approved minutes of the General Assembly, annual reports and audited accounts;
- (d) be treated fairly, with dignity and without discrimination;
- (e) raise concerns and grievances under Articles 34 and 36; and
- (f) resign at any time by written notice.

12.2 **Founder and Ordinary Members additionally have the right to:**
- (a) vote at General Assembly meetings;
- (b) be nominated for, elect and be elected to the Board and other offices;
- (c) propose motions and resolutions; and
- (d) requisition an Extraordinary General Meeting under Article 22.5.

12.3 **Every Member is obliged to:**
- (a) uphold and abide by this Constitution, the policies and the Code of Conduct;
- (b) promote the vision, mission, objectives and reputation of the Foundation;
- (c) pay any prescribed subscription promptly;
- (d) attend General Assembly meetings or send apologies;
- (e) declare any conflict of interest under Article 31;
- (f) protect confidential information;
- (g) comply with safeguarding requirements, including any required screening, before engaging with children or vulnerable persons;
- (h) refrain from making any representation, incurring any obligation, or entering any commitment on behalf of the Foundation without written authority; and
- (i) refrain from any conduct likely to bring the Foundation into disrepute.

12.4 No Member shall by reason only of membership acquire any interest in the property or assets of the Foundation.

## ARTICLE 13 — CESSATION, SUSPENSION AND EXPULSION

13.1 **Membership shall terminate upon:**
- (a) written resignation delivered to the Secretary;
- (b) death, or in the case of an institutional member, dissolution or winding-up;
- (c) failure to pay a prescribed subscription within six (6) months of it falling due, after two written reminders, and following a Board resolution;
- (d) expulsion for conduct deemed detrimental to the aims of the Foundation, under Article 13.3;
- (e) the Member becoming of unsound mind as certified by a competent medical practitioner, or being declared bankrupt or insolvent; or
- (f) conviction of an offence involving fraud, dishonesty, violence, or any offence against a child or vulnerable person.

13.2 **Suspension.** Where an allegation of serious misconduct is made, the Board may suspend a Member's rights pending investigation for up to ninety (90) days, extendable once by Board resolution. Suspension is precautionary and is not a finding of guilt. Where the allegation concerns safeguarding, suspension from all contact with children shall be immediate and mandatory.

13.3 **Expulsion.** A Member may be expelled by resolution of not less than two-thirds (2/3) of the Board, ratified by the General Assembly, where the Member has:
- (a) materially breached this Constitution, a policy, or the Code of Conduct;
- (b) acted in a manner prejudicial to the interests, reputation or safety of the Foundation, its beneficiaries or its Personnel;
- (c) misappropriated, misused or wilfully damaged the funds or property of the Foundation;
- (d) committed an act of harassment, exploitation, abuse or violence; or
- (e) persistently failed to fulfil the obligations of membership.

13.4 **Due process.** No Member shall be expelled unless:
- (a) given written particulars of the allegations;
- (b) afforded not less than fourteen (14) days to respond in writing and an opportunity to be heard in person, accompanied by a person of their choice; and
- (c) the Board has considered the response before deciding.

13.5 **Appeal.** An expelled Member may appeal in writing to the General Assembly within thirty (30) days. The appeal shall be heard at the next General Assembly, whose decision by Special Resolution shall be final.

13.6 **Consequences.** A person whose membership ceases shall:
- (a) forfeit all rights, privileges and claims against the Foundation and its property;
- (b) not be entitled to any refund of subscriptions or contributions;
- (c) immediately surrender all property, records, data, keys, credentials and identification belonging to the Foundation; and
- (d) remain bound by continuing obligations of confidentiality and by any liability incurred while a Member.

13.7 A former Member may apply for readmission after twelve (12) months, save that a person removed for an offence against a child or vulnerable person shall be **permanently ineligible** for membership or engagement in any capacity.

## ARTICLE 14 — REGISTER OF MEMBERS

14.1 The Secretary shall maintain a **Register of Members** (Template L) recording for each Member: full name, membership number, category, nationality, contact particulars, date of admission, subscriptions paid, and date and reason of cessation.

14.2 The Register shall be updated within thirty (30) days of any change and shall be available for inspection by any Member at the headquarters at reasonable times on reasonable notice.

14.3 Personal data in the Register shall be processed in accordance with Article 35 and shall not be disclosed to third parties without consent, save where required by law or by the Commission.

# PART IV — GOVERNANCE ORGANS

## ARTICLE 15 — ORGANS OF THE ORGANIZATION

15.1 The organs of the Foundation shall be:
- **(a) The General Assembly** — the supreme governing body;
- **(b) The Board of Directors** — the governing and oversight body responsible for policy-making and strategic direction;
- **(c) Committees of the Board** — specialized organs exercising delegated functions;
- **(d) The Secretariat** — the executive arm responsible for day-to-day running; and
- **(e) The Advisory Council** — a non-binding advisory organ.

15.2 **Separation of roles.** The General Assembly and the Board exercise governance and oversight; the Secretariat exercises management and implementation. No Board member shall interfere in day-to-day management otherwise than through the Executive Director.

---

## ARTICLE 16 — THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY

16.1 The **General Assembly** is the supreme governing body of the Foundation and comprises all registered Members in good standing. It meets annually at the **Annual General Meeting (AGM)**.

16.2 **Functions and powers.** The General Assembly shall:
- (a) receive, consider and adopt the annual report, audited financial statements and auditor's report;
- (b) elect, and where necessary remove, the elected members of the Board;
- (c) determine the broad policy direction and strategic priorities;
- (d) approve the Strategic Plan and any material change to it;
- (e) appoint the independent external auditor and determine the auditor's remuneration;
- (f) approve amendments to this Constitution by Special Resolution;
- (g) approve new categories of membership and the level of subscriptions;
- (h) ratify expulsions and hear appeals under Articles 11.3 and 13.5;
- (i) approve the disposal, mortgage or encumbrance of immovable property;
- (j) confer Honorary Membership;
- (k) resolve upon dissolution and the distribution of assets; and
- (l) exercise any other power conferred on it by this Constitution or by law.

16.3 The AGM shall be held at least once in every calendar year, and not more than fifteen (15) months after the preceding AGM.

16.4 **Business of the AGM** shall include:
- (a) confirmation of the minutes of the previous meeting;
- (b) the Chairperson's report;
- (c) the Executive Director's report on programs and operations;
- (d) the Treasurer's report and presentation of audited accounts;
- (e) the nationalization compliance report under Article 30.7;
- (f) appointment or reappointment of the auditor;
- (g) elections, where due; and
- (h) any other business of which due notice has been given.

16.5 The General Assembly may meet in **Special General Meeting** (also referred to as an Extraordinary General Meeting) in accordance with Article 22.5.

16.6 Meetings may be held physically, virtually, or in hybrid form, provided all participating Members can hear and be heard and votes can be reliably recorded. Given the Foundation's international affiliation and the realities of travel within South Sudan, provision shall always be made for remote participation.

---

## ARTICLE 17 — THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS

17.1 **Composition.** The Board of Directors is the governing and oversight body responsible for policy-making and strategic direction. It shall consist of not fewer than **five (5)** and not more than **nine (9)** members elected by the General Assembly, including the Chairperson, Vice-Chairperson, Secretary and Treasurer, together with:
- (a) the **Founder**, who shall be a member of the Board for life or until resignation or removal for cause;
- (b) up to two (2) members co-opted by the Board for specific skills or stakeholder representation, counted within the maximum of nine; and
- (c) the **Executive Director**, who shall attend *ex officio* without the right to vote.

17.2 **Composition principles.** In constituting the Board, the General Assembly shall have regard to the need for:
- (a) a **majority of members who are South Sudanese nationals**;
- (b) a majority of members independent of management and of any material financial relationship with the Foundation;
- (c) not less than one-third (1/3) of members being of either gender;
- (d) representation reflecting the ethnic and geographic diversity of the areas of operation;
- (e) a mix of competencies including STEM education, financial management, law, safeguarding, fundraising and community development; and
- (f) at least one member with recognized financial or accounting expertise, to serve on the Finance and Audit Committee.

17.3 **Eligibility.** A person is eligible for the Board if that person:
- (a) is eighteen (18) years of age or above;
- (b) is of sound mind and has not been declared bankrupt or insolvent;
- (c) has not been convicted of an offence involving fraud, dishonesty or moral turpitude, nor of any offence against a child or vulnerable person;
- (d) is not a serving member of the armed forces or an organized force, nor the holder of a political office, unless they have resigned or taken leave;
- (e) is not disqualified from serving as a director or trustee under any applicable law; and
- (f) subscribes in writing to this Constitution, the Code of Conduct and the Safeguarding Policy.

17.4 **Term.** Elected Board members serve a term of **three (3) years**, renewable for not more than **two (2) further consecutive terms**, after which a full term must elapse before re-eligibility. Terms shall be staggered so that approximately one-third of the Board retires each year.

17.5 **Functions and powers.** The Board shall:
- (a) hold the Foundation's assets and act as fiduciary for its mission and beneficiaries;
- (b) set policy and strategic direction, and approve the annual budget, work plan and annual report;
- (c) appoint, supervise, appraise, discipline and, where necessary, remove the Executive Director and set the terms of service;
- (d) approve organizational policies, including finance, procurement, human resources, safeguarding, security, data protection, risk management, whistleblowing and investment policies;
- (e) oversee financial management, internal controls, risk and compliance;
- (f) ensure compliance with the Act, the terms of the Foundation's registration, the nationalization requirement in Article 30, and all reporting obligations to the Commission and to donors;
- (g) approve the opening and closing of bank accounts and designate signatories;
- (h) approve contracts, grants and commitments above thresholds delegated to management;
- (i) establish committees and delegate functions, while retaining ultimate responsibility;
- (j) admit Members and recommend expulsions to the General Assembly;
- (k) co-opt Board members and fill casual vacancies under Article 17.8;
- (l) implement the resolutions and policy direction of the General Assembly; and
- (m) exercise all powers not reserved to the General Assembly.

17.6 **Duties of Board members.** Every Board member shall:
- (a) act honestly, in good faith, and in the best interests of the Foundation and its beneficiaries;
- (b) exercise the care, skill and diligence of a reasonably prudent person in a comparable position;
- (c) avoid conflicts of interest and disclose them under Article 31;
- (d) not use their position, or information obtained through it, for personal gain;
- (e) attend meetings regularly and prepare adequately;
- (f) maintain confidentiality; and
- (g) support Board decisions publicly once lawfully taken, notwithstanding a dissenting vote, and record any dissent in the minutes.

17.7 **Cessation of Board membership.** A Board member ceases to hold office upon:
- (a) expiry of term without re-election;
- (b) written resignation to the Chairperson (or, for the Chairperson, to the Vice-Chairperson);
- (c) ceasing to be eligible under Article 17.3;
- (d) absence from three (3) consecutive Board meetings without reasonable cause accepted by the Board;
- (e) removal by Special Resolution of the General Assembly for material breach of duty, misconduct, incapacity, or conduct prejudicial to the Foundation, after written particulars and a fair opportunity to be heard; or
- (f) death.

17.8 **Casual vacancies.** The Board may appoint a qualified person to fill a casual vacancy, subject to ratification at the next General Assembly, to serve the unexpired portion of the predecessor's term.

17.9 **Indemnity.** Every Board member and Office Bearer shall be indemnified out of the assets of the Foundation against liability reasonably incurred in the proper discharge of their duties, **provided that** no indemnity shall extend to liability arising from fraud, dishonesty, wilful misconduct, gross negligence, or acts knowingly outside the powers of the Foundation.

17.10 **Frequency of meetings.** The Board shall meet at least **four (4) times** in each Financial Year, at intervals of not more than four (4) months.

## ARTICLE 18 — DUTIES OF OFFICE BEARERS

18.1 The Office Bearers of the Foundation shall be the **Chairperson**, the **Vice-Chairperson**, the **Secretary** and the **Treasurer**, elected by the General Assembly or, where the General Assembly so directs, by the Board from among its members, each for a term concurrent with their term as a Board member.

18.2 **The Chairperson** shall:
- (a) convene and preside over all General and Board meetings;
- (b) act as the official spokesperson for the Foundation;
- (c) provide leadership to the Board and ensure its effectiveness;
- (d) serve as the line manager of the Executive Director on behalf of the Board;
- (e) sign minutes, resolutions and instruments as authorized; and
- (f) exercise a **casting vote** in the event of an equality of votes.

18.3 **The Vice-Chairperson** shall deputise for the Chairperson in their absence or incapacity and perform such functions as the Chairperson or Board may assign.

18.4 **The Secretary** shall:
- (a) record and keep minutes of all meetings;
- (b) handle official correspondence of the Foundation;
- (c) maintain the register of members, the register of Board members, and all statutory records;
- (d) convene meetings on the instruction of the Chairperson and issue notices and agendas;
- (e) act as custodian of the Constitution, the common seal and all legal documents; and
- (f) ensure returns and notifications to the Commission and other authorities are filed on time.

18.5 **The Treasurer** shall:
- (a) oversee all financial operations of the Foundation;
- (b) ensure proper bookkeeping and the maintenance of internal controls;
- (c) present financial reports at the AGM and the audited accounts to the General Assembly;
- (d) present the draft annual budget to the Board;
- (e) chair the Finance and Audit Committee;
- (f) monitor the Foundation's financial position and report any material concern to the Board without delay; and
- (g) serve as a signatory to the Foundation's bank accounts.

18.6 The Board may appoint any other Office Bearer it considers necessary and define that Office Bearer's functions.

18.7 An Office Bearer may be removed from office (though not necessarily from the Board) by resolution of two-thirds (2/3) of the Board, after notice and an opportunity to be heard.

## ARTICLE 19 — COMMITTEES OF THE BOARD

19.1 The Board may establish standing and *ad hoc* committees, define their terms of reference, appoint their members (who need not all be Board members), and dissolve them.

19.2 **Standing Committees** shall ordinarily include:

**(a) Finance and Audit Committee** — chaired by the Treasurer; oversees budgeting, financial reporting, internal controls, procurement, risk, the external audit and management's response to audit findings.

**(b) Programs Committee** — oversees program design, quality, monitoring, evaluation and learning; reviews program reports and beneficiary outcomes.

**(c) Scholarships and Awards Committee** — establishes and applies transparent criteria for selecting scholarship recipients; reviews and approves awards; monitors recipient progress and welfare.

**(d) Safeguarding Committee** — oversees the Child and Vulnerable Persons Safeguarding Policy, receives and oversees the handling of safeguarding reports, and reports directly to the Board.

**(e) Governance, Nominations and Ethics Committee** — oversees Board composition and succession, induction and evaluation, conflicts of interest, and the Code of Conduct.

**(f) Human Resources and Nationalization Committee** — oversees recruitment, terms of service, capacity building, and compliance with the 80% nationalization requirement in Article 30.

**(g) Resource Mobilization and Partnerships Committee** — oversees fundraising strategy, donor stewardship, ethical acceptance of gifts, and partnership development.

19.3 Every committee shall have written terms of reference specifying composition, quorum, frequency of meetings and reporting lines, and shall report to the Board at each ordinary Board meeting.

19.4 The Board may delegate any of its functions to a committee, **save that** it shall not delegate: approval of the annual budget or audited accounts; appointment or removal of the Executive Director; approval of policies; disposal of immovable property; or any matter reserved to the Board or the General Assembly by this Constitution or by law. Delegation does not relieve the Board of ultimate responsibility.

## ARTICLE 20 — THE SECRETARIAT AND MANAGEMENT

20.1 The Secretariat is the executive arm responsible for the day-to-day running of the Foundation, headed by the **Executive Director** (also styled Secretary-General).

20.2 **The Executive Director** shall be appointed by the Board on such terms as the Board determines, and shall:
- (a) implement the strategic plan, policies and resolutions of the Board;
- (b) lead the development of annual work plans and budgets for Board approval;
- (c) manage the Foundation's Personnel, finances, assets and operations within approved budgets and delegated authority;
- (d) represent the Foundation before the Commission, government ministries, partners and donors;
- (e) ensure compliance with law, donor conditions and Foundation policies, including the nationalization requirement;
- (f) report to the Board at each meeting on programs, finance, risk, security and safeguarding; and
- (g) be accountable to the Board through the Chairperson.

20.3 **Other positions.** The Secretariat may include, as resources permit, a Deputy Director, Programs Manager, Program Coordinators, Finance Officer, Human Resources Officer, Monitoring and Evaluation Officer, Communications Officer, Security Focal Point, Safeguarding Focal Person, and such other positions as the Board approves in the staff establishment.

20.4 **Safeguarding Focal Person.** The Board shall designate a Safeguarding Focal Person who shall receive safeguarding reports, maintain the safeguarding register, and have a direct and unobstructed reporting line to the Chairperson of the Safeguarding Committee.

20.5 **Recruitment.** Appointments shall be made on merit through a fair, transparent and competitive process, advertised so as to reach South Sudanese candidates, and consistent with Article 30. All Personnel with access to children or vulnerable persons shall undergo background screening and reference checks before engagement and shall sign the Safeguarding Undertaking (Template I).

20.6 **Volunteers.** Volunteers, mentors and interns shall be engaged under a written Volunteer Agreement (Template J), shall be subject to the Code of Conduct and Safeguarding Policy, and shall be supervised by a designated member of Personnel. Volunteers are not employees and shall not be entitled to remuneration, save for the reimbursement of approved expenses.

20.7 **Terms and conditions.** All Personnel shall be engaged under written contracts consistent with the **Labour Act, 2017** of the Republic of South Sudan and the Foundation's Human Resources Policy.

20.8 No member of the Secretariat shall be a voting member of the Board.

## ARTICLE 21 — ADVISORY COUNCIL

21.1 The Board may constitute an **Advisory Council** of distinguished persons in STEM, education, philanthropy, industry or public service, to provide strategic, technical and reputational support.

21.2 Members of the Advisory Council shall be appointed by the Board for renewable terms of two (2) years, shall serve voluntarily and honorarily, and shall have **no fiduciary duty, decision-making power, or voting rights**, their role being purely advisory.

21.3 The Advisory Council shall meet at least once a year and may submit recommendations to the Board, which the Board shall consider but shall not be bound to adopt.

# PART V — MEETINGS AND DECISION-MAKING

## ARTICLE 22 — MEETINGS, NOTICE AND QUORUM

22.1 **Notice.**

| Meeting | Minimum notice |
|---|---|
| Annual General Meeting | 21 clear days in writing |
| Special / Extraordinary General Meeting | 14 clear days in writing |
| General Meeting to pass a Special Resolution (incl. amendment) | 21 clear days, specifying the intention to propose the resolution and its terms |
| General Meeting to consider dissolution | 21 clear days, expressly stating the purpose |
| Ordinary Board meeting | 14 clear days |
| Emergency Board meeting | 48 hours, with reasons stated |
| Committee meeting | 7 clear days |

22.2 Notice shall state the date, time, place and/or means of virtual access, accompanied by the agenda and, so far as practicable, the supporting papers. Notice may be given by hand, post, electronic mail, or such other means as the Board approves.

22.3 The accidental omission to give notice to, or the non-receipt of notice by, any person entitled to receive it shall not invalidate the proceedings.

22.4 **Quorum.**

| Meeting | Quorum |
|---|---|
| General Assembly | One-half (1/2) of Members entitled to vote, or fifteen (15) such Members, whichever is fewer |
| General Meeting considering dissolution | As required by Article 40.1 |
| Board of Directors | One-half (1/2) of Board members in office, including at least one Office Bearer, and a majority of those present being South Sudanese nationals |
| Committee | One-half (1/2) of committee members, including the chairperson or their nominee |

22.5 **Special General Meeting.** A Special General Meeting may be convened:
- (a) by resolution of the Board;
- (b) by the Chairperson, where urgent business requires it; or
- (c) upon a written requisition signed by not less than one-third (1/3) of Members entitled to vote, stating the business to be transacted.

Where a valid requisition is delivered to the Secretary, the meeting shall be convened within twenty-one (21) days. Should the Board fail to do so, the requisitionists may convene it within a further thirty (30) days, and the Foundation shall meet the reasonable costs.

22.6 **Adjournment for want of quorum.** If a quorum is not present within one (1) hour of the appointed time, the meeting stands adjourned to the same day in the following week at the same time and place (or as the Chairperson directs). At the adjourned meeting, those present shall constitute a quorum, **provided that** no Special Resolution, no amendment to this Constitution, and no resolution for dissolution shall be passed at such an adjourned meeting.

22.7 **Chairing.** Meetings shall be chaired by the Chairperson; in their absence by the Vice-Chairperson; and failing both, by a member chosen by those present.

22.8 **Virtual and hybrid meetings.** Any meeting may be held wholly or partly by electronic means. Participants so attending count for quorum and may vote. The Secretary shall ensure reliable identification of participants and accurate recording of votes.

22.9 **Written resolutions.** A resolution in writing (including by counterpart or electronic signature) signed by all Board members entitled to vote shall be as valid as a resolution passed at a duly convened Board meeting, and shall be recorded in the minutes of the next meeting. This does not apply to the General Assembly.

## ARTICLE 23 — VOTING AND RESOLUTIONS

23.1 Each Member entitled to vote shall have **one (1) vote**. There shall be no plural voting.

23.2 Voting shall be by show of hands, unless a secret ballot is demanded by the chairperson of the meeting or by not less than one-fifth (1/5) of those present and entitled to vote. **Elections to the Board and votes on removal from office shall be by secret ballot.**

23.3 Save where this Constitution or the law requires a greater majority, all questions shall be decided by **Ordinary Resolution**.

23.4 **Matters requiring a Special Resolution** (two-thirds majority of members present and voting):
- (a) amendment of this Constitution;
- (b) removal of a Board member;
- (c) ratification of expulsion of a Member, and determination of appeals under Article 13.5;
- (d) disposal, mortgage or encumbrance of immovable property;
- (e) change of the name of the Foundation; and
- (f) affiliation to, merger with, or amalgamation with another organization.

23.5 **Dissolution** requires a **three-quarters (3/4) majority** in accordance with Article 40.1.

23.6 **Casting vote.** In the event of an equality of votes, the chairperson of the meeting shall have a second or casting vote, save on a vote concerning that chairperson's own removal, office or interest, where the motion shall be deemed lost.

23.7 **Proxies.** A Member entitled to vote may appoint another Member as proxy by written instrument (Template E) delivered to the Secretary not less than twenty-four (24) hours before the meeting. No Member shall hold more than two (2) proxies. Proxies are not permitted at Board meetings; Board members shall attend and vote in person or by electronic participation.

23.8 A Member in arrears of subscription for more than six (6) months, or under suspension, shall not vote.

23.9 No person shall vote on any matter in which they have a conflict of interest, and shall withdraw from the meeting for the duration of that item, in accordance with Article 31.

## ARTICLE 24 — MINUTES AND RECORDS

24.1 Minutes of every meeting of the General Assembly, the Board and every committee shall be recorded, and shall include: date, time and place; names of those present and apologies; declarations of interest; a summary of deliberations; every decision and resolution; recorded dissents; and action points with responsible persons and deadlines.

24.2 Draft minutes shall be circulated within fourteen (14) days and confirmed at the next meeting, whereupon they shall be signed by the chairperson of that meeting and countersigned by the Secretary.

24.3 Confirmed minutes shall be *prima facie* evidence of the proceedings recorded.

24.4 The Foundation shall keep at its headquarters, and retain for not less than **ten (10) years** (or longer where law or donor agreements require):
- (a) this Constitution and all amendments;
- (b) certificates of registration and all permits;
- (c) the Register of Members and register of Board members;
- (d) minute books of all organs;
- (e) books of account, vouchers and audited financial statements;
- (f) the Asset Register;
- (g) contracts, grant agreements and memoranda of understanding;
- (h) personnel records and the nationalization staffing returns; and
- (i) the safeguarding register and incident records.

24.5 Records shall be kept securely, with backups of electronic records maintained in a separate location, and shall be accessible for inspection by the Commission, the auditor and any person authorized by law.

# PART VI — FINANCE AND PROPERTY

## ARTICLE 25 — SOURCES OF FUNDS

25.1 The Foundation shall be funded by:
- (a) membership dues, entrance fees and contributions;
- (b) grants from foundations, trusts, corporations, governments, development partners, United Nations agencies and multilateral bodies;
- (c) donations, gifts, bequests and endowments from individuals, whether within South Sudan or abroad;
- (d) fundraising activities, events, appeals and campaigns;
- (e) online and offline giving, including electronic payment platforms operated in accordance with applicable payment and data-security standards;
- (f) income from investments and interest on deposits;
- (g) proceeds of activities carried on in furtherance of the Foundation's objectives; and
- (h) any other lawful source approved by the Board.

25.2 **Ethical acceptance of funds.** The Board shall adopt a Gift Acceptance Policy. The Foundation shall decline any donation or grant which:
- (a) derives from, or is reasonably suspected to derive from, unlawful activity;
- (b) is offered on conditions that conflict with the Foundation's objectives, values, independence, humanitarian principles or safeguarding obligations;
- (c) would require the Foundation to act in a partisan political manner or to favour any ethnic or political constituency;
- (d) would expose the Foundation to unacceptable legal, financial or reputational risk; or
- (e) cannot be lawfully accepted under applicable sanctions, anti-money-laundering or charity laws.

25.3 **Restricted funds** shall be applied strictly in accordance with the donor's stated purpose, accounted for separately, and reported on as the donor agreement requires. Any proposed variation requires the donor's prior written consent.

25.4 All funds received shall be **promptly banked** in an account of the Foundation and shall not be applied to any purpose other than the objectives of the Foundation.

25.5 The Foundation shall issue an official receipt for every donation received and maintain records sufficient to satisfy donors, auditors and the Commission.

25.6 **Foreign funding.** All funding received from outside the Republic of South Sudan shall be received through the Foundation's registered bank accounts, recorded in the books of account, and disclosed to the Commission and other competent authorities as the Act requires.

---

## ARTICLE 26 — FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT AND CONTROLS

26.1 The Board shall adopt and periodically review a **Finance and Accounting Policy** and a **Procurement Policy** consistent with this Constitution, applicable law and donor requirements.

26.2 The Foundation shall maintain proper books of account in accordance with **International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS)**, or such other standard as the Board on the auditor's advice approves, permitting separate reporting of restricted and unrestricted funds.

26.3 **Minimum internal controls.** The Foundation shall at all times observe:
- (a) **segregation of duties** — the persons who authorize, execute and record a transaction shall be different persons;
- (b) **dual authorization** — every payment shall require at least two authorized signatories;
- (c) **budgetary control** — no expenditure outside an approved budget without prior written Board or delegated approval;
- (d) **documentation** — every disbursement supported by an approved requisition, invoice or receipt;
- (e) **competitive procurement** — goods and services above thresholds set by the Board procured through documented competitive processes;
- (f) **asset control** — all assets recorded, tagged, insured where appropriate, and physically verified at least annually;
- (g) **petty cash limits** — petty cash operated on an imprest system within Board-set limits;
- (h) **bank reconciliation** — accounts reconciled monthly and reviewed by a person other than the preparer;
- (i) **currency management** — a written policy on holding and converting foreign currency, recording exchange rates used and the source of each rate; and
- (j) **cash handling** — no cash payment above the threshold set by the Board save in documented exceptional circumstances, and a written protocol for the secure movement and custody of cash in field locations.

26.4 **Delegated authority.** The Board shall approve a written schedule of delegated financial authority specifying the monetary limits within which the Executive Director and other officers may commit the Foundation. Commitments above those limits require prior Board approval.

26.5 **Reporting.** Management shall present to the Board at each ordinary meeting: management accounts, budget-versus-actual analysis, cash-flow position, donor grant utilization, and a register of significant risks.

26.6 **Prohibition on personal use.** No funds or property shall be applied to the personal use or benefit of any Member, Board member or member of Personnel. Loans and advances to Board members are prohibited. Salary advances to Personnel may be made only under a written policy approved by the Board.

---

## ARTICLE 27 — BANK ACCOUNTS AND SIGNATORIES

27.1 Accounts shall be opened in the name of "Jdiobe STEM Foundation" with **reputable commercial banks in South Sudan** licensed by the Bank of South Sudan and approved by the Board. Accounts may additionally be held in other jurisdictions where necessary for donor receipts, subject to Board approval and disclosure to the Commission.

27.2 The Board shall by resolution designate not fewer than **four (4) signatories**, of whom at least one shall be an Office Bearer of the Board and a majority shall be South Sudanese nationals resident in the country.

27.3 Every instrument for the withdrawal or transfer of funds shall be signed or authorized by **any two (2) of the designated signatories**, of whom at least one shall be a Category A signatory as designated by the Board.

27.4 No signatory shall sign a blank cheque or a blank payment instruction.

27.5 A signatory who ceases to hold their office or position shall be removed as a signatory within **fourteen (14) days**, and the Secretary shall notify the bank accordingly.

27.6 Electronic and mobile-money channels may be used where authorized by the Board, subject to equivalent dual-authorization controls, transaction limits, and monthly reconciliation.

27.7 Payment-processing credentials, API keys and administrative access to any online donation platform shall be held under the control of the Executive Director, subject to access logs and periodic review by the Finance and Audit Committee.

---

## ARTICLE 28 — FINANCIAL YEAR, ACCOUNTS AND AUDIT

28.1 The **Financial Year** shall run from **1 January to 31 December**, save that the first Financial Year shall run from the date of adoption of this Constitution to the following 31 December.

28.2 Within **three (3) months** of the close of each Financial Year, the Executive Director shall cause financial statements to be prepared, comprising a statement of financial position, a statement of income and expenditure, a statement of cash flows, a statement of changes in funds, and notes distinguishing restricted from unrestricted funds.

28.3 The accounts shall be **audited annually by an independent, certified external auditor appointed by the General Assembly**, who shall be independent of the Board, the Personnel and any material donor.

28.4 The auditor shall be appointed annually on the recommendation of the Finance and Audit Committee. The same audit firm shall not serve for more than **six (6) consecutive years** without rotation of the engagement partner.

28.5 The auditor shall have unrestricted access to all books, records, vouchers, assets, Personnel and premises, and shall be entitled to attend and be heard at the General Assembly on matters concerning the audit.

28.6 The audited financial statements, the auditor's report and the management letter shall be:
- (a) considered by the Finance and Audit Committee and the Board;
- (b) presented to the AGM for adoption;
- (c) submitted to the Relief and Rehabilitation Commission and any other authority within the time prescribed by law;
- (d) submitted to donors as their agreements require; and
- (e) published in summary form in the Foundation's annual report.

28.7 The Board shall respond in writing to every material finding in the management letter and shall track implementation of agreed remedial actions.

---

## ARTICLE 29 — PROPERTY AND ASSETS

29.1 All property of the Foundation, movable and immovable, shall be held in the name of the Foundation and applied solely to the promotion of its objectives.

29.2 The Secretary shall maintain an **Asset Register** (Template M) recording for each asset: description, identification or serial number, date and cost of acquisition, funding source, location, custodian, condition, depreciation and date of disposal.

29.3 A physical verification of assets shall be conducted at least annually and reconciled to the Asset Register, with discrepancies reported to the Board.

29.4 **Acquisition and disposal.**
- (a) Acquisition or lease of immovable property requires prior Board approval and compliance with the law governing land holding by non-governmental organizations.
- (b) Disposal, mortgage, charge or encumbrance of immovable property requires a Special Resolution of the General Assembly.
- (c) Disposal of movable assets shall follow the Asset Disposal Policy, by transparent means, with proceeds banked to the Foundation's account.
- (d) Assets acquired with restricted donor funds shall be dealt with in accordance with the applicable grant agreement, including on termination of the grant.

29.5 **Vehicles and equipment.** Vehicles, generators, communications and laboratory equipment shall be logged, fuelled and maintained under written procedures, with usage logs reviewed monthly. Private use of Foundation vehicles is prohibited save as expressly authorized in writing.

29.6 **Intellectual property.** All curricula, research outputs, publications, software, designs, photographs, databases and other intellectual property created by Personnel or contractors in the course of their engagement shall vest in the Foundation, unless otherwise agreed in writing. The Foundation shall, where consistent with its mission and donor terms, make educational materials available under open licences for public benefit.

29.7 **Use of images and data of beneficiaries.** No image, name, testimony or personal story of a beneficiary shall be published without informed, documented consent — and, for a child, the consent of a parent or guardian together with the assent of the child. Consent may be withdrawn at any time, and no benefit shall be conditioned upon it. Images shall never disclose identifying location information where doing so could place a beneficiary at risk.

# PART VII — EMPLOYMENT AND ACCOUNTABILITY

## ARTICLE 30 — EMPLOYMENT AND STAFFING (NATIONALIZATION REQUIREMENT)

30.1 **Statutory requirement.** The Foundation shall comply with the Non-Governmental Organizations Act, 2016 by ensuring that **not less than eighty percent (80%) of its employees are South Sudanese nationals across managerial, middle and junior levels**.

30.2 **Expatriate engagement.** Expatriate staff shall only be engaged where there are **no qualified South Sudanese nationals available** for the role. Before any expatriate appointment, the Foundation shall:
- (a) advertise the position locally through channels reasonably calculated to reach qualified South Sudanese candidates, for not less than fourteen (14) days;
- (b) document the recruitment process, the applications received, and the reasoned assessment that no qualified national candidate was available;
- (c) obtain the approval of the Executive Director and, for managerial positions, of the Board;
- (d) obtain all work permits, visas and approvals required by law; and
- (e) retain the documentation for inspection by the Commission and the auditor.

30.3 **Level-by-level compliance.** The eighty percent threshold shall be met at **each** of the managerial, middle and junior levels, and not merely in aggregate across the workforce. The Human Resources and Nationalization Committee shall monitor compliance at each level quarterly.

30.4 **Succession and skills transfer.** Every expatriate appointment shall carry a documented **succession and skills-transfer plan** identifying:
- (a) the national understudy or counterpart designated for the role;
- (b) the specific competencies to be transferred;
- (c) the training, mentoring and delegation milestones; and
- (d) the target date for the transfer of the role to a national.

The plan shall be reviewed at each contract renewal, and renewal shall not be granted where the plan has been neglected without good cause.

30.5 **Capacity building.** The Foundation shall allocate a budget line each year for the training and professional development of national staff, and shall report annually on the training delivered.

30.6 **Terms of service.** All Personnel shall be engaged under written contracts consistent with the **Labour Act, 2017**, specifying job title, duties, duration, remuneration, hours, leave, benefits, disciplinary and grievance procedures, and termination. Equal pay shall be given for work of equal value, and no distinction in benefits shall be made between national and expatriate staff other than those objectively justified by the terms of international assignment.

30.7 **Reporting.** The Executive Director shall present a **Nationalization Compliance Return** (Template N) to the Board quarterly and to the AGM annually, showing the national/expatriate ratio at each level, the reason for each expatriate post, and progress against succession plans. The return shall be submitted to the Commission as required.

30.8 **Remedial action.** Where the Foundation falls below the eighty percent threshold at any level, the Board shall adopt a written remedial plan within thirty (30) days, specifying the steps and timeline to restore compliance, and shall notify the Commission.

30.9 **Recruitment integrity.** Recruitment shall be conducted on merit through a documented, competitive process. Solicitation or acceptance of any payment or favour in exchange for employment is gross misconduct and shall be reported to the competent authorities.

## ARTICLE 31 — CONFLICT OF INTEREST

31.1 A conflict of interest arises where the personal, financial, family, ethnic, professional or other interest of a Member, Board member or member of Personnel conflicts, or may reasonably be perceived to conflict, with the interests of the Foundation.

31.2 Every Board member, Office Bearer and senior member of Personnel shall complete a **Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form** (Template C) upon appointment and annually thereafter, and shall update it promptly upon any change.

31.3 Any person with a conflict of interest in a matter before a meeting shall:
- (a) declare the nature and extent of the interest at the earliest opportunity and before the matter is discussed;
- (b) not participate in the discussion, save to answer questions if invited;
- (c) withdraw from the meeting for the duration of that item; and
- (d) not vote, and not be counted in the quorum for that item.

31.4 Every declaration and the action taken shall be recorded in the minutes and in a **Register of Interests** maintained by the Secretary.

31.5 **Related-party transactions.** Any transaction between the Foundation and a Board member, Office Bearer, member of Personnel, or a connected person or entity, shall be:
- (a) disclosed in full;
- (b) approved in advance by the disinterested members of the Board;
- (c) demonstrably on terms no less favourable to the Foundation than arm's-length terms; and
- (d) disclosed in the annual financial statements.

31.6 **Nepotism.** No person shall participate in the recruitment, appraisal, promotion, discipline, or determination of remuneration of a relative, nor in the selection of a relative as a beneficiary. Any familial relationship between Personnel, Board members and beneficiaries shall be declared.

31.7 **Founder conflicts.** Where a matter concerns the Founder's own interests, remuneration, or performance, the Founder shall recuse themself and the Vice-Chairperson or an independent Board member shall preside.

31.8 Failure to disclose a material conflict constitutes misconduct and may result in removal from office or membership.

## ARTICLE 32 — CHILD AND VULNERABLE PERSONS SAFEGUARDING

32.1 The Foundation works directly with children and young people. **The safety, welfare and dignity of children and vulnerable persons is the paramount consideration of the Foundation and takes precedence over the Foundation's reputation, funding, programs and any other interest.**

32.2 The Board shall adopt, publish and review annually a **Child and Vulnerable Persons Safeguarding Policy** consistent with the **Child Act, 2008**, the Penal Code Act, 2008, the Transitional Constitution, and international good practice including the Inter-Agency Standing Committee standards on protection from sexual exploitation and abuse.

32.3 **Minimum standards.** The Foundation shall ensure that:
- (a) every member of Personnel, Board member, volunteer, mentor and partner in contact with children is screened, referenced and, where lawful and practicable, subject to criminal-record checks before engagement;
- (b) every such person signs the **Safeguarding Undertaking** (Template I) and receives safeguarding induction and refresher training;
- (c) a **Safeguarding Focal Person** is designated and their contact details are known to beneficiaries, Personnel and communities;
- (d) no adult is alone and unobserved with a child, save in an emergency, and a **two-adult rule** applies to all activities involving children;
- (e) programs are risk-assessed for safeguarding before delivery, including transport, residential activities and field visits;
- (f) children and communities are informed, in accessible language and in the local languages of the area, of how to raise a concern; and
- (g) accessible, confidential reporting channels are maintained and publicized.

32.4 **Prohibited conduct.** It is prohibited for any person acting for the Foundation to:
- (a) engage in sexual activity with, or sexually exploit or abuse, any child or beneficiary, regardless of the local age of majority or consent;
- (b) exchange money, employment, goods, services, assistance or benefits for sexual favours or any other form of exploitation;
- (c) use physical, humiliating or degrading punishment or discipline;
- (d) develop a relationship with a child that could be deemed exploitative or abusive;
- (e) use the Foundation's devices, networks or premises to access, produce or distribute child sexual abuse material; or
- (f) act in any way intended to shame, humiliate, belittle or degrade a child or vulnerable person.

32.5 **Reporting.** Any person who becomes aware of, or suspects, a safeguarding concern shall report it to the Safeguarding Focal Person **within twenty-four (24) hours**. The Focal Person shall notify the Chairperson of the Safeguarding Committee immediately, and the Board shall be informed of every serious incident without delay.

32.6 **Response.** The Foundation shall respond to every report by: ensuring the immediate safety of the child; making any mandatory referral to police, the Ministry of Gender, Child and Social Welfare, and other competent authorities as required by law; suspending the subject of the allegation from all contact with children pending investigation; conducting a fair investigation; and providing or facilitating survivor support, including medical and psychosocial referral.

32.7 **No retaliation and no cover-up.** No person shall be penalized for making a report in good faith. Any attempt to suppress, discourage or conceal a safeguarding report is itself gross misconduct.

32.8 A person found to have committed an offence against a child or vulnerable person shall be **summarily dismissed or expelled**, shall be permanently ineligible for any role in the Foundation, and shall be reported to the competent authorities.

32.9 The Board shall receive a safeguarding report at every ordinary meeting, including the number and nature of concerns raised and their resolution, and shall record safeguarding as a standing agenda item.

## ARTICLE 33 — ANTI-CORRUPTION, ANTI-FRAUD AND ANTI-MONEY-LAUNDERING

33.1 The Foundation maintains a **zero-tolerance** position on bribery, corruption, fraud, embezzlement, theft, extortion, favouritism and money laundering.

33.2 No person acting for the Foundation shall offer, give, solicit or accept any bribe, kickback, facilitation payment, or improper advantage, whether directly or through an intermediary, and whether to a public official or a private person.

33.3 The Foundation shall comply with the anti-corruption and anti-money-laundering laws of the Republic of South Sudan, shall cooperate with the South Sudan Anti-Corruption Commission, and shall observe applicable international sanctions regimes and the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act in respect of its affiliated office.

33.4 **Gifts and hospitality.** Personnel and Board members shall not accept gifts, hospitality or benefits from suppliers, partners, beneficiaries or donors beyond items of nominal value as defined in the Board's Gifts Policy, and all such items shall be recorded in a Gifts Register.

33.5 **Due diligence.** The Foundation shall conduct proportionate due diligence on significant donors, partners, suppliers and sub-grantees, including screening against applicable sanctions and terrorist-financing lists, and shall retain records of such screening.

33.6 Any suspected fraud or corruption shall be reported immediately to the Chairperson and the Finance and Audit Committee, investigated promptly, and where substantiated shall result in disciplinary action, recovery of losses and, where appropriate, referral to law enforcement.

## ARTICLE 34 — WHISTLEBLOWER PROTECTION

34.1 The Board shall adopt a **Whistleblowing Policy** providing accessible, confidential and, where requested, anonymous channels through which any person may report suspected wrongdoing, including fraud, corruption, safeguarding violations, discrimination, harassment, or breach of this Constitution.

34.2 Reports may be made to the Executive Director, the Safeguarding Focal Person, the Chairperson of the Finance and Audit Committee, or the Chairperson of the Board. Where the report concerns one of these persons, it shall be made to another of them.

34.3 The identity of a whistleblower shall be protected so far as the law and the requirements of a fair investigation permit.

34.4 **No person shall suffer dismissal, demotion, harassment, exclusion or any other detriment for making a report in good faith**, even where the report is not ultimately substantiated. Retaliation is itself gross misconduct.

34.5 Knowingly making a false or malicious report constitutes misconduct.

34.6 Every report shall be acknowledged within seven (7) days, assessed, and where warranted investigated, with the outcome recorded and reported in summary form to the Board.

## ARTICLE 35 — DATA PROTECTION AND PRIVACY

35.1 The Foundation processes personal data of students, families, donors, Personnel and partners, and shall handle it lawfully, fairly and securely, in accordance with the Transitional Constitution's protection of privacy, any applicable data-protection legislation, and donor requirements.

35.2 The Foundation shall:
- (a) collect personal data only for specified, lawful purposes connected to its objectives, and only to the extent necessary;
- (b) obtain informed consent where required, and for children, the consent of a parent or guardian;
- (c) keep personal data accurate, secure and up to date;
- (d) restrict access to personal data to those who require it for their duties;
- (e) retain personal data only as long as necessary or as required by law or donor agreement, and thereafter securely destroy it;
- (f) not sell, rent or trade personal data;
- (g) apply appropriate technical and organizational safeguards, including encryption of sensitive data and access controls; and
- (h) impose equivalent obligations on processors and partners by written agreement.

35.3 **Heightened sensitivity.** Given the operating context, data revealing a person's ethnicity, displacement status, political opinion, or location shall be treated as highly sensitive, minimized, and disclosed only where strictly necessary, having regard to the risk of harm to the data subject.

35.4 **Donor and payment data.** The Foundation shall not itself store full payment-card data. Electronic donations shall be handled through PCI-DSS-compliant payment processors, and access to processor dashboards and credentials shall be restricted and logged.

35.5 **Cross-border transfers.** Transfers of personal data between the South Sudan operation and the affiliated U.S. office shall be limited to what is necessary, subject to appropriate safeguards, and disclosed in the Foundation's privacy notice.

35.6 **Breach notification.** Any suspected personal-data breach shall be reported internally without delay, assessed, and notified to affected persons and competent authorities where required by law or where necessary to prevent harm.

35.7 Data subjects shall be entitled to access, correction, and, where applicable, erasure of their personal data, through a documented request procedure.

36.1 The Foundation shall provide a working and learning environment free from discrimination, harassment, sexual harassment, bullying, ethnic hostility and victimization.

36.2 Sexual harassment, sexual exploitation and abuse are gross misconduct warranting summary dismissal or expulsion, and where they constitute criminal offences shall be reported to the competent authorities.

36.3 The Board shall adopt a **Grievance and Disciplinary Policy**, consistent with the Labour Act, 2017, providing for:
- (a) informal resolution where appropriate;
- (b) written complaint, investigation by an impartial person, and a right to be heard;
- (c) the right to be accompanied at any disciplinary or grievance hearing;
- (d) proportionate sanctions ranging from warning to dismissal;
- (e) a right of appeal to a person or body not involved in the original decision, and ultimately to the Board; and
- (f) defined timelines for each stage.

36.4 **Beneficiary complaints.** The Foundation shall maintain an accessible complaints mechanism for students, parents, schools and communities, publicize it in the languages of the communities served, and ensure that making a complaint never prejudices a beneficiary's access to services.

36.5 A summary of complaints received and their resolution shall be reported to the Board at each ordinary meeting.

# PART VIII — GENERAL

## ARTICLE 38 — DISPUTE RESOLUTION

38.1 The Foundation and its Members shall endeavour to resolve every dispute internally, amicably and in good faith, having regard to the mission of the Foundation and the interests of its beneficiaries.

38.2 **Stage 1 — Internal resolution.** A dispute shall first be referred in writing to the Executive Director or, where the Executive Director is a party, to the Chairperson, who shall attempt resolution within thirty (30) days.

38.3 **Stage 2 — Board determination.** If unresolved, the dispute shall be referred to the Board, which may appoint a panel of not fewer than three (3) disinterested persons to hear the parties and report. The Board shall determine the matter within sixty (60) days.

38.4 **Stage 3 — Mediation.** If still unresolved, the parties shall refer the dispute to mediation by a mediator agreed between them or, failing agreement, appointed by [INSERT NOMINATING BODY, e.g. the South Sudan Chamber of Commerce, Industry and Agriculture].

38.5 **Stage 4 — Arbitration or courts.** Any dispute not resolved by mediation within sixty (60) days shall be referred to arbitration by a single arbitrator, the seat being **Juba**, the language English, and the award final and binding; or, where arbitration is not available or appropriate, to the competent courts of the Republic of South Sudan.

38.6 Nothing in this Article shall:
- (a) prevent any party from seeking urgent interim relief from a court of competent jurisdiction;
- (b) delay or impede the reporting of a criminal offence or a safeguarding concern to the competent authorities, which shall be immediate and unconditional; or
- (c) limit the jurisdiction of the Relief and Rehabilitation Commission or any other regulator.

38.7 This Constitution shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Republic of South Sudan.

## ARTICLE 39 — AMENDMENT OF THE CONSTITUTION

39.1 This Constitution may be amended by a **two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of members present and voting** at an Annual General Meeting or a Special General Meeting, **provided that** notice of the proposed amendments has been circulated at least **twenty-one (21) days** prior to the meeting.

39.2 A proposed amendment may be initiated by:
- (a) the Board; or
- (b) a written proposal signed by not less than one-third (1/3) of Members entitled to vote, delivered to the Secretary.

39.3 The notice of proposed amendment shall set out the existing text, the proposed text, and the reasons for the change.

39.4 **No amendment shall be made that would:**
- (a) cause the Foundation to cease to be a non-profit, non-partisan, charitable organization;
- (b) permit the distribution of income or assets to Members otherwise than as permitted by Article 9.3;
- (c) alter Article 40 (Dissolution) so as to permit distribution of assets to Members;
- (d) reduce the nationalization requirement in Article 30 below the statutory threshold; or
- (e) weaken the safeguarding protections in Article 32 below the standards required by law or international good practice.

39.5 Every amendment shall be:
- (a) recorded in the **Constitutional Amendment Record** (Template P), showing the article amended, the previous text, the new text, the resolution number and the date;
- (b) submitted to the Relief and Rehabilitation Commission for approval or notification as required by law; and
- (c) effective from the date of the resolution or, where regulatory approval is required, from the date of that approval.

39.6 The Secretary shall issue a consolidated, re-dated version of the Constitution after each amendment and distribute it to all Members and Board members.

## ARTICLE 40 — DISSOLUTION AND DISTRIBUTION OF ASSETS

40.1 The Foundation may be dissolved by a resolution passed by a **three-quarters (3/4) majority vote of members present** at a Special General Meeting convened for that purpose, of which not less than twenty-one (21) clear days' notice expressly stating that purpose has been given.

40.2 The Foundation may also be dissolved by order of a court of competent jurisdiction, or upon cancellation of its registration by the Relief and Rehabilitation Commission in accordance with law.

40.3 Upon a resolution to dissolve:
- (a) the Board shall immediately notify the Commission, all relevant authorities and all donors;
- (b) the Foundation shall cease all activities except those necessary for winding up;
- (c) the General Assembly shall appoint not fewer than three (3) liquidators or a winding-up committee, including at least one person independent of the Board; and
- (d) a final audit shall be conducted.

40.4 **Order of application of assets.** The assets shall be applied in the following order:
1. settlement of all lawful debts, liabilities and the costs of winding up;
2. settlement of all obligations to Personnel, including terminal benefits under the Labour Act, 2017;
3. return to donors of unspent restricted funds, or their application as the relevant grant agreements direct;
4. discharge of obligations to beneficiaries then in receipt of multi-year scholarships, so far as the assets permit, the Board being obliged to make every reasonable effort to secure the continuity of those students' education, including by transfer of the commitment to a successor organization.

40.5 **Surplus assets.** After all debts and liabilities have been paid, the remaining assets and property **shall not be distributed among the Members**, Board members or Personnel of the Foundation. They shall be transferred to another legally recognized organization with similar objectives in the Republic of South Sudan, which prohibits the distribution of its income and property among its members to an extent at least as great as is imposed by this Constitution, chosen by the General Assembly at or before the time of dissolution and approved by the Relief and Rehabilitation Commission.

40.6 If no such organization can be identified, the assets shall be applied to a charitable purpose consistent with the objectives of the Foundation as the Commission or a court of competent jurisdiction may direct.

40.7 The liquidators shall render a final report and statement of account to the General Assembly and the Commission, and shall lodge the records of the Foundation with a person or institution able to retain them for the period required by law.

## SCHEDULE 2 — PROGRAM DESCRIPTIONS

The Foundation delivers a connected STEM development pathway spanning early exposure through university and career entry.

### 2.1 Youth STEM School Program — *Inspire*
Hands-on STEM workshops for primary and middle-school learners, designed to spark early curiosity through building, experimenting and discovery. Delivered in partnership with schools; includes teacher orientation and provision of learning materials.

### 2.2 Community STEM Outreach — *Engage*
Workshops, demonstrations, science fairs and community events delivered with schools, local leaders and community partners, extending STEM learning beyond the classroom and engaging parents and communities in supporting learners.

### 2.3 Secondary School Research Program — *Develop*
Mentored student research and innovation projects that build critical thinking, experimental design, data literacy and problem-solving. Includes robotics, environmental engineering and water-quality monitoring strands, culminating in exhibitions and competitions.

### 2.4 STEM Scholarships — *Support*
Financial support, mentorship and leadership development for academically promising, financially disadvantaged students pursuing STEM education. Named scholarship funds currently include:
- the **Dr. Ron and Cara Beer STEM Scholarship**; and
- the **Regina Henry Scholarship**.

Each named fund shall be governed by written terms recording the donor's intent, eligibility criteria, award value, duration, renewal conditions and reporting requirements, approved by the Board and administered by the Scholarships and Awards Committee.

### 2.5 Aerospace Institute
A specialized program and facility promoting aerospace and applied engineering education, reflecting the Founder's professional discipline, including model aircraft, propulsion and flight-science modules and industry exposure.

### 2.6 Mentorship and Career Readiness
Structured matching of students with engineers, scientists, educators and industry professionals nationally and internationally, supported by career-guidance sessions, industry visits and university-application support. All mentors are screened and bound by the Safeguarding Policy.

**Program governance.** Every program shall have: a written concept note and logframe with defined outcomes and indicators; an approved budget; safeguarding and security risk assessments; a monitoring, evaluation and learning plan; and a designated responsible officer. Program performance shall be reported to the Programs Committee quarterly and to the Board at each ordinary meeting.

## TEMPLATE H — CODE OF CONDUCT AND UNDERTAKING

> **JDIOBE STEM FOUNDATION — CODE OF CONDUCT**
> *Applicable to all Members, Board members, employees, volunteers, mentors, interns, consultants and partners*

**1. PURPOSE**
This Code sets the standard of behaviour expected of every person acting in the name of the Foundation. It supplements, and does not replace, the Constitution, the Safeguarding Policy and applicable law.

**2. I WILL:**
- (a) act with honesty, integrity and impartiality at all times;
- (b) treat every student, colleague, partner and community member with respect and dignity, regardless of ethnicity, gender, religion or origin;
- (c) put the safety and welfare of children and vulnerable persons above all other considerations;
- (d) use the Foundation's funds, property, vehicles, information and name only for authorized purposes;
- (e) declare any conflict of interest promptly and fully;
- (f) keep accurate records and report truthfully;
- (g) protect confidential and personal information;
- (h) comply with the laws of the Republic of South Sudan and of every country in which I act for the Foundation;
- (i) observe all security procedures and instructions;
- (j) report any concern about wrongdoing or safeguarding promptly through the proper channels;
- (k) respect the cultures, beliefs and customs of the communities we serve; and
- (l) cooperate fully with any investigation.

**3. I WILL NOT:**
- (a) engage in any sexual activity with, or exploitation or abuse of, any child or beneficiary;
- (b) offer, solicit or accept any bribe, kickback or improper benefit, including in exchange for employment or a scholarship;
- (c) misappropriate, misuse or falsify the Foundation's funds, property or records;
- (d) discriminate against, harass, bully or victimize any person;
- (e) use physical, humiliating or degrading discipline;
- (f) consume or be under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs while working with children or representing the Foundation;
- (g) carry or permit a weapon on the Foundation's premises or in its vehicles;
- (h) use the Foundation's name, resources or platforms for partisan political purposes, ethnic mobilization, or personal gain;
- (i) make unauthorized statements to the media or on social media on behalf of the Foundation;
- (j) photograph, film or publish any beneficiary without documented consent; or
- (k) retaliate against any person who raises a concern in good faith.

**4. BREACH**
